10 Thanos Moments That Deserved To Be In The MCU

At this point Thanos is a household name, up there with Darth Vader and Voldemort in the hall of well-known villains. This is almost entirely due to his stellar portrayal in the MCU, which arguably did a better job with Thanos than a lot of his comics.

That being said, the films can be a bit limiting for poor Thanos, with the movies being too family friendly for some of his darker moments, and a bit too serious for his wackier moments.

But that doesn't mean it can't be done! And now that everyone and their grandma knows who Thanos is, it's the perfect time to bring him back from the dead and put some of his best (and possibly worst) moments on the big screen. Lets have Josh Brolin fly a tiny yellow helicopter or help old ladies across the street! He could lose a fight to squirrels and torture one specific guy named Dave!

So (for any Marvel executives reading) here are the perfect Thanos moments from across the comics that DESPERATELY need some screen time.


10. That Time He Made Deadpool Immortal Out Of Spite

Unlike his MCU counterpart, comic Thanos loves Death. And not just inflicting it, Thanos is in love with the literal personification of Death. The issue is Death doesn’t love him back; she only has eyes for one Wade "merc with a mouth" Wilson, AKA Deadpool.

So like any good incel Thanos spends his time throwing temper tantrums and complaining Deadpool isn’t good enough for her (which in this case might actually be true). Not that it makes a difference though - Death is a grown cosmic entity and can make her own decisions. Thanos, however, isn't the most progressive when it comes to cosmic romance, and in the pages of Deadpool #64, finally decides to do something about his romantic rival, cursing Deadpool with immortality so he and Death can never be together.

So how would this look on screen? Well we already know Ryan Reynolds and Josh Brolin work well together, as demonstrated by their partnership in Deadpool 2, and a romantic comedy where Deadpool and Thanos compete for the affections of a literal skeleton is the movie we all need to combat Marvel fatigue.


9. That Time He Fought Commando Galactus

What lies at the very end of the Marvel universe? What is still standing long after everything else is dead? Thanos, obviously. In the Thanos Wins storyline we get an insight into what the Marvel universe looks like when Thanos has succeeded in killing everything.

After he kills all the heroes of Earth, Thanos turns his attentions to the cosmic beings that inhabit the universe, and this of course means Galactus. In response, the big purple world eater and his new partner the Cosmic Ghost Rider (Frank Castle), arm themselves up in true commando style and go to hunt down Thanos.

Needless to say, it does not go in their favour.

With rumours circling about Galactus making an appearance in Phase 4, what better way to introduce him than a John Wick style shootout with Thanos? It's the action movie we all deserve. .


8. That Time He Defeated A Cosmic Pantheon

Beating Galactus not enough for you? How about beating Galactus and all the cosmic gods at the same time?

Everyone knows that when Thanos got his hands on the Infinity Gauntlet he used it to eliminate half of all life in the universe, but in the pages of the Infinity Gauntlet comic he used the stones for so much more.

From manifesting grand structures in the vacuum of space, to creating elaborate tortures for his enemies, Thanos was truly creative with his godlike power. But most impressive is how he used the gauntlet to fight a pantheon of cosmic beings, including Galactus, Lord Chaos and Master Order alongside a few others, simultaneously.

What we have here is the opportunity for an epic Endgame style conflict, but on a truly cosmic scale. With massive entities hitting each other with planets, while Thanos and the embodiment of time throw each other into dinosaurs, what more does anyone need? .


7. That Time He Lost To Squirrel Girl

With all the immense strength Thanos has shown, it's sometimes easy to forget he isn’t the most powerful character in the Marvel canon. No, that honour goes to none other than Squirrel Girl.

That’s right, the girl with the proportionate speed and strength of a squirrel has a proven track record of beating some of Marvel's heaviest hitters, including Doctor Doom, Galactus and of course Thanos.

The two clash in GLX Christmas special, in which Thanos arrives and threatens to drain the life of everyone on Earth. Squirrel Girl jumps into battle and defeats the Mad Titan off panel, cheating audiences out of seeing Thanos fighting an army of squirrels.

This one sells itself, to be honest. Thanos, the planet conquering demigod, taken out by a teenager and an army of squirrels. .


6. That Time He Helped An Old Lady Across The Street

Quite possibly the most deviously evil thing Thanos has ever done was helping an old lady across the street. How is this evil you might ask? Well, that’s a bit of a story. In the annual issue of Thanos's comic series, we are treated to a series of lesser known Thanos stories - one of which occurred when Thanos had the Infinity Gauntlet.

With the time stone allowing him to see infinite potential futures, Thanos hatched a plan.

One of the futures Thanos saw involved a young woman from Earth. This young lady had a bright future. She was going to cure to disease, end hunger and usher in a new age of peace across the universe. And it all starts with a chance meeting getting off a bus. Something Thanos prevents by delaying the bus through helping an old lady across the street.

To add insult to injury when this poor woman is on her deathbed, having lived a mediocre life thanks to Thanos, the Mad Titan appears and shows her everything she would have accomplished if not for him.

We have all seen what Thanos can do on grand scale, so let's get some screen time dedicated to his cruelty on a small scale. .


5. That Time He Put All Of Existance In A Chokehold

As mentioned earlier, Thanos and Deadpool are romantic rivals - both competing for the affections of Death herself. So in Deadpool vs Thanos, when Death goes missing, who better to team up and find her? As a newly formed gruesome twosome, Thanos and Deadpool travel the galaxy to rescue their lost love.

The pair eventually find out that she has been kidnapped by Eternity itself, AKA the living embodiment of the Marvel universe. Thanos sees his opportunity to strike and with a little help from Death, grabs eternity and literally tries to choke the life out of him.

And he would've gotten away with it too if it wasn't for that meddling Deadpool and his newly acquired Captain Universe powers!

This one doesn't even need selling as a movie. Thanos wrestles the concept of existence and Deadpool gets magical universe powers - it writes itself.


4. That Time He Became The Punisher

Thanos has had a lot of titles in his day, but one title I don’t think anyone expected him to get was The Punisher.

Thanos is the end of all things and as they say, strange times lead to strange bedfellows. Towards the end of his life, Thanos finds himself with the Cosmic Ghost Rider as his right hand man, who just so happens to be Frank Castle, the original Punisher.

After Castle and Galactus failed to defeat Thanos, Cosmic Ghost Rider was spared so he could serve Thanos and provide him with a penance stare each morning. Eventually Castle is killed and thanks to some antics from Odin gets the opportunity to go back in time to rehabilitate Thanos, something that is described as the worst decision ever made in all living history.

In the pages of Cosmic Ghost Rider we see this plan in action and it of course goes badly, and only ends up turning Thanos into a version of The Punisher.

Terrible for the universe, but great for us, a gritty Netflix drama about The Punisher already exists and it's great. A gritty Netflix drama about Thanos The Punisher should exist and would (probably) be even better.


3. That Time He Had The Thanos Copter

In this Super Spidey story, Thanos gets his hands on the cosmic cube - you know, that thing that can alter reality on a whim? And he still somehow manages to lose, and not even to the Avengers or anything big; he is thwarted by Spider Man, Hellcat and a small child.

After Thanos just drops the cube (for the second time) a young boy picks it up and uses it to tie Thanos up, after which two regular police officers just arrest him with no issue.

But as entertaining as that scene would be on screen, even better would be the tiny yellow helicopter Thanos pilots to get the cube. You all the know the one - it has his name on the side so he doesn’t lose it. .


2. That Time He Tortured Dave

We have seen Thanos commit cosmic genocide on a semi regular basis, and with that it's easy to overlook the fact that Thanos always makes time for the little evils. However, even if its it’s the smallest things, Thanos still does it better than anyone else.

A prime example is his torture of a perfectly regular man named David. Every year on poor David’s birthday Thanos shows up and tries to ruin David’s life, starting on little Dave's first birthday when Thanos stole his blanket.

From there, the Mad Titan made sure these annual traditions were the worst days of David's life. Killing Dave's father, friends and cat, breaking up with his girlfriend and flooding his house; no evil is too much or too little for Thanos.

All we need now is a slice of life series where Thanos occasionally shows up and makes everything worse, then disappears again until next season. .


1. That Time He Killed His Future Self

As you know from his fight with Galactus, the Thanos Wins story line shows him as the end of all things - you might even say he is “inevitable”. However, this does come with a downside. As an immortal and having killed everyone else in the universe, Thanos has no one left to kill him.

So who better to do the Job than Thanos himself? Old man Thanos literally pulls a younger version of himself from the past to come and kill him in the future. Something young Thanos does, not through murder, but by changing the past so old man Thanos never existed in the first place. Because Thanos does no man’s dirty work - not even his own

Hugh Jackman already proved that fighting a younger version of yourself in a movie works flawlessly with Logan. Now all we need is the same thing done in the MCU. Bring Thanos back and have him fight himself! .
